Couser Overview

Core and Elective Subjuect

The General Printing program begins with core subjects that cover the foundational principles of printing technology, including techniques such as offset printing, screen printing, digital printing, and flexography. Students will learn about the printing process from start to finish, including prepress (design, typesetting), printing, and post-press (binding, finishing). Essential subjects also cover color theory, typography, and material handling. Elective subjects allow students to specialize in areas like packaging design, label printing, and graphic design, enabling them to cater to specific industries such as commercial printing, publishing, and advertising. The diverse electives empower students to master skills that align with their career aspirations.

In-house training

Hands-on training is a key part of the program, where students work with industry-standard printing presses, graphic design software, and finishing equipment. In the school’s print shops, students gain practical experience in creating print materials like brochures, flyers, business cards, and posters. They practice the entire printing workflow, from designing layouts to operating printing presses, performing quality control, and managing printing materials. This practical exposure builds the technical skills necessary to work in the printing industry, helping students understand machine operations, color consistency, and material handling, all while practicing safe and efficient printing techniques.

Work Place Experience learning

: Workplace experience is provided through collaborations with local printing companies, giving students the opportunity to apply their classroom knowledge in real-world environments. During these placements, students work on live print jobs under professional supervision, gaining experience with commercial printing operations such as large-scale production runs, print job scheduling, and troubleshooting equipment issues. This experience exposes them to the fast-paced nature of the printing industry, where meeting deadlines and managing customer expectations are key. By working alongside industry professionals, students gain valuable skills, build a network with potential employers, and enhance their technical abilities and work ethic.

Certification

Upon completion of the program, students earn certifications recognized by Ghana’s Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) framework. The National Proficiency I and II certifications focus on basic skills in printing, including prepress work and operating printing machines. The National Certificate I and II offer more advanced training in printing technology and operations. For students pursuing supervisory roles, the Higher National Diploma (HND) program prepares graduates for managerial positions in the printing industry, including print production management and quality control. These certifications are valued both locally and internationally, allowing graduates to pursue career opportunities in printing businesses worldwide.

Job Opportunities

: Graduates of the General Printing program have a variety of career opportunities across different sectors. Entry-level positions such as Printing Assistant, Press Operator, and Prepress Technician enable new graduates to hone their skills in different stages of the printing process. With additional experience and higher-level certifications, roles expand to positions like Print Production Supervisor, Quality Control Inspector, and Graphic Designer, where attention to detail, creativity, and knowledge of print technologies are essential. The printing industry offers opportunities in sectors such as publishing, advertising, packaging, and custom printing services, with potential for high-paying jobs and career advancement.

Things to Know Before Choosing the Course

This course requires creativity, attention to detail, and manual dexterity, as students will be working with printing machinery and materials. Students must be prepared to work in a physically demanding environment, where operating printing presses, managing materials, and ensuring quality control are critical. Safety knowledge is important, as printing environments often involve the use of chemicals, machinery, and heavy equipment. Students should also be prepared for continuous learning, as printing technologies evolve with the advent of digital printing and other innovations. This course is ideal for individuals with an interest in design, color, and the printed medium, and a desire to pursue a career in a dynamic, creative industry.
