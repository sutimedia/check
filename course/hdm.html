Couser Overview

Core and Elective Subjuect

The Heavy Duty Mechanical program begins with core subjects that establish foundational skills essential for working on heavy machinery, including diesel engines, hydraulic systems, and powertrains. Students gain knowledge in essential safety protocols, machine diagnostics, and technical blueprint reading, enabling them to troubleshoot and maintain equipment such as loaders, excavators, and heavy trucks. Elective subjects allow for specialization in areas such as advanced hydraulics, electronic control systems, and heavy vehicle maintenance. This flexibility enables students to tailor their training toward specific industries, including mining, construction, and transportation, while providing a strong base of high-demand skills.

In-house training

Hands-on, in-house training is a critical aspect of the Heavy Duty Mechanical program. In workshops equipped with industry-standard tools like hydraulic jacks, diagnostic scanners, and specialized torque equipment, students work on projects that replicate real-world tasks, such as engine overhauls, hydraulic pump repairs, and transmission maintenance. This structured environment allows students to become proficient in handling complex machinery under safe, supervised conditions. The practical projects foster confidence in managing heavy equipment, emphasize precision and safety, and instill best practices essential for industrial work. This training ensures that students are well-prepared to transition into demanding work environments smoothly.

Work Place Experience learning

To reinforce in-class learning, the program includes workplace experience opportunities with partner companies in heavy industries. During these placements, students apply their skills to actual job sites, performing mechanical maintenance, diagnostics, and repairs on-site under the guidance of professional mechanics and engineers. This real-world experience exposes students to the rigors of heavy-duty mechanical work, including working in rugged environments, meeting operational deadlines, and coordinating with team members. By working alongside industry experts, students gain valuable insights, develop connections with potential employers, and further refine both their technical and professional skills.

Certification

Upon program completion, students earn certifications recognized within Ghana’s Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) framework. The National Proficiency I and II certifications validate essential skills like basic machinery operation and maintenance. National Certificates I and II prepare students for more complex responsibilities, while the Higher National Diploma (HND) equips graduates for roles involving supervision or specialization in heavy equipment repair and maintenance. These certifications are respected in both Ghana and international markets, enabling graduates to pursue career opportunities locally or abroad with credentials that indicate their technical competency and professional commitment.

Job Opportunities

Graduates of the Heavy Duty Mechanical program have various job opportunities across multiple sectors. Entry-level roles, such as Heavy Equipment Technician, Maintenance Assistant, and Field Mechanic, provide hands-on experience. With additional experience and certifications, graduates can advance to roles like Maintenance Supervisor, Hydraulic Systems Specialist, or Equipment Inspector, where knowledge of machinery maintenance, quality assurance, and mechanical troubleshooting is essential. There are further opportunities in industries such as mining, construction, and agriculture, where expertise in heavy machinery and specialized repair skills can lead to high-paying and stable career paths.
