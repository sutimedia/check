Course Overview

Core and Elective Subjects

The Welding and Fabrication program begins with core subjects that focus on fundamental skills, including basic welding techniques such as Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW), Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (TIG), and Gas Metal Arc Welding (MIG). Students learn essential safety protocols, metallurgy principles, and blueprint reading to interpret complex designs and translate them into metal structures. Elective subjects allow specialization in areas such as advanced TIG welding, ornamental metalworking, and pipe welding, tailoring the training to diverse sectors like automotive, aerospace, or structural engineering. The variety of electives supports career goals by offering both breadth and depth, empowering students to master high-demand skills.

In-house Training

Hands-on, in-house training is a vital component, where students operate industry-grade tools, including MIG welders, angle grinders, cutting torches, and CNC plasma cutters. Within the school’s workshops, students undertake real projects that simulate industry tasks, such as fabricating small structures, frames, and pipes. This environment allows them to practice material handling, precise cuts, and assembly techniques under safe, supervised conditions. The projects provide practical exposure to welding processes, build confidence in handling equipment, and instill meticulous safety practices, crucial in industrial work. Furthermore, this internal training ensures students are equipped to transition smoothly into work environments.

Workplace Experience Learning

Workplace experience is provided through collaborations with local businesses, giving students exposure to actual job sites and industry settings. During these placements, students apply classroom-learned skills in real-world environments, engaging in welding tasks on construction sites, factories, or repair workshops under professional supervision. This on-the-job learning exposes them to the operational demands of welding and fabrication, such as meeting tight deadlines, communicating with team members, and managing materials effectively. By working alongside industry professionals, students gain valuable insights, network with potential employers, and enhance both their technical abilities and professional confidence

Certification

Upon completing the program, students earn various qualifications recognized by Ghana’s Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) framework. The National Proficiency I and II certifications introduce basic skill recognition for tasks like metal assembly and fabrication. National Certificates I and II advance skills in more complex areas, while the Higher National Diploma (HND) prepares students for supervisory roles or specialized fields within welding and fabrication. Each certification is progressively valued and recognized both in Ghana and internationally, enabling graduates to pursue jobs domestically or abroad with respected credentials that signal competence and dedication to potential employers.

Job Opportunities

Graduates from the program have numerous employment opportunities across various sectors. Entry-level positions such as Fabrication Assistant, Welding Technician, and Welder’s Helper allow new graduates to develop skills on the job. With experience and higher certifications, roles expand to Fabrication Specialist, Structural Welder, and Welding Inspector, where skills in technical expertise, quality assurance, and process management are essential. Opportunities also exist in industries like oil and gas, shipbuilding, and aerospace, where expertise in precision welding and specialized techniques can lead to high-paying positions and career advancement within international companies.

Things to Know Before Choosing the Course

This course requires physical stamina, hand dexterity, and attention to detail, as welding can be demanding and precise. Safety knowledge is paramount; understanding risks and maintaining a commitment to safety protocols are essential for preventing accidents. Students should also be prepared for continuous learning as welding technology evolves. The course is ideal for those with an interest in creating tangible, lasting products and a readiness to commit to a skilled trade that offers varied and evolving career paths. Additionally, considering long-term alignment with career goals is crucial, as this field often requires dedication to skill mastery over years.
