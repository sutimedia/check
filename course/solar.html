Couser Overview

Core and Elective Subjuect

The Solar Panel Installation program begins with core subjects that cover the essential principles of solar energy, including photovoltaic (PV) system design, solar panel installation, electrical wiring, and energy storage solutions. Students will learn about solar energy theory, energy efficiency, the components of solar systems (such as inverters, batteries, and mounting systems), and electrical safety protocols. Elective subjects allow students to specialize in areas such as grid-tied and off-grid solar systems, solar thermal systems, energy management, and advanced installation techniques. These electives cater to industries such as residential, commercial, and industrial solar power installations, empowering students to acquire both fundamental and specialized skills, positioning them for careers in renewable energy.

In-house training

Hands-on, in-house training is a crucial aspect of the program, where students work with industry-standard tools and equipment used for solar panel installation. In the training labs and workshops, students gain practical experience by installing, wiring, and testing photovoltaic systems, learning how to assess solar energy requirements, select appropriate components, and troubleshoot system issues. They also practice safe installation techniques and familiarize themselves with local codes and regulations for solar energy systems. Students work on real projects that simulate industry tasks, providing them with the technical expertise needed to handle both residential and commercial solar installations. This practical training ensures they are fully prepared to transition smoothly into the workforce upon completion of the program.

Work Place Experience learning

: Workplace experience is offered through partnerships with local solar installation companies, where students gain exposure to real-world job sites. During these placements, students apply their classroom learning in actual installations, including mounting solar panels, wiring systems, configuring inverters, and connecting to the electrical grid. Students also observe industry best practices in system troubleshooting, maintenance, and performance monitoring. These hands-on experiences allow students to work alongside experienced professionals, providing them with a deeper understanding of the challenges and requirements of solar panel installation. Workplace learning also offers valuable networking opportunities and helps students develop the practical skills needed to succeed in the renewable energy industry

Certification

Upon successful completion of the program, students earn qualifications recognized by Ghana’s Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) framework. The National Proficiency I and II certifications validate essential skills in solar panel installation, including basic electrical work, system assembly, and safety protocols. National Certificates I and II advance students’ expertise in solar energy systems, including energy efficiency practices, system troubleshooting, and advanced installation techniques. The Higher National Diploma (HND) prepares graduates for supervisory roles or specialized fields, such as system design, renewable energy consulting, or project management. The certifications are recognized both in Ghana and internationally, providing graduates with the credentials to pursue opportunities in the rapidly growing global renewable energy market.

Job Opportunities

Graduates of the Solar Panel Installation program have numerous employment opportunities within the expanding renewable energy sector. Entry-level positions such as Solar Installation Technician, Solar Panel Installer, and Electrical Technician allow graduates to gain hands-on experience working in residential, commercial, and industrial solar projects. With additional certifications and experience, graduates can move into higher-level roles such as Solar Project Manager, Solar Systems Designer, and Energy Consultant, where expertise in system design, project management, and energy optimization is critical. The demand for renewable energy solutions, particularly solar power, is growing globally, offering graduates the potential for high-paying positions in both domestic and international markets.

Things to Know Before Choosing the Course

The Solar Panel Installation program requires physical stamina, attention to detail, and a passion for renewable energy. Students should be comfortable working outdoors and in various weather conditions, as solar installations often take place on rooftops or in open areas. A strong foundation in mathematics and electrical principles is important, as students will need to understand system sizing, electrical wiring, and energy calculations. Safety knowledge is paramount; students must be aware of potential electrical hazards, proper lifting techniques, and fall protection measures. Additionally, students should be prepared for continuous learning as solar technology evolves and new industry standards emerge. This course is ideal for those who are passionate about renewable energy, sustainability, and working with cutting-edge technology that has a positive environmental impact.
