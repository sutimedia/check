Couser Overview

Core and Elective Subjuect

The Automotive Technology program begins with core subjects that focus on the fundamental skills required to diagnose, repair, and maintain vehicles. Students will learn engine mechanics, electrical systems, brake systems, suspension systems, air conditioning, and transmission systems, as well as how to interpret technical manuals and work with diagnostic tools. Elective subjects allow students to specialize in areas such as hybrid vehicle technology, automotive electrical diagnostics, or advanced engine performance, offering a tailored education that prepares them for the rapidly evolving automotive industry. These electives provide both depth and breadth, empowering students to develop high-demand skills in various aspects of automotive repair and maintenance.

In-house training

Hands-on, in-house training is an essential part of the program. Students work with industry-standard diagnostic tools, automotive lifts, engine simulators, and other equipment in fully equipped workshops. In these workshops, students perform real-world tasks such as servicing engines, diagnosing electrical faults, repairing brake systems, and conducting routine maintenance on a variety of vehicles. This environment allows students to gain practical experience working on vehicles, giving them the skills needed to manage real automotive challenges. In addition to technical proficiency, students develop problem-solving skills, attention to detail, and a strong understanding of safety procedures necessary for working with complex machinery and systems.

Work Place Experience learning

: Workplace experience is provided through partnerships with local automotive repair shops, dealerships, and service centers. During these placements, students apply their classroom knowledge in real-world environments, working on actual vehicle repairs under the supervision of experienced technicians. This practical exposure includes tasks such as troubleshooting engine performance issues, conducting routine maintenance, and assisting with customer service. By interacting with professionals in the field, students gain valuable insights into the operational aspects of the automotive industry, build connections with potential employers, and gain the hands-on experience required to boost their employability.

Certification

Upon completion of the program, students receive certifications recognized by Ghana’s Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) framework. The National Proficiency I and II certifications provide foundational recognition for tasks such as engine diagnostics and basic repair work. National Certificates I and II cover more advanced technical skills, such as advanced diagnostics, air conditioning systems, and hybrid vehicle technologies. The Higher National Diploma (HND) prepares students for managerial roles or specialized positions in automotive repair and maintenance, such as service manager or automotive technician. These certifications are internationally recognized, giving graduates the opportunity to pursue employment both locally and globally with respected credentials.

Job Opportunities

Graduates from the Automotive Technology program have a wide range of career opportunities in various sectors. Entry-level positions such as Automotive Technician, Service Assistant, and Diagnostic Specialist allow new graduates to develop hands-on skills and gain valuable experience. With further certifications and experience, roles expand to positions such as Service Manager, Automotive Diagnostic Specialist, and Workshop Supervisor, where expertise in vehicle systems, repair management, and customer service is essential. The automotive industry offers opportunities in vehicle dealerships, independent repair shops, fleet management, and even in specialized fields such as electric vehicle (EV) technology, providing graduates with high-paying and dynamic career paths.

Things to Know Before Choosing the Course

This course requires physical stamina, problem-solving abilities, and strong attention to detail, as automotive repair can involve manual labor and troubleshooting complex systems. Students should be prepared for working with tools, machinery, and sometimes heavy components. Safety knowledge is critical, as working with vehicles often involves dealing with hazardous materials, such as oils, lubricants, and high-voltage electrical systems. Additionally, as automotive technologies continue to evolve, students should expect to stay updated on the latest advancements in vehicle systems, including hybrid and electric vehicle technologies. This course is ideal for individuals who enjoy hands-on work, problem-solving, and have a keen interest in cars and automotive systems, and it offers a career path with long-term potential for growth and development.
