Couser Overview

Core and Elective Subjuect

The Catering and Hospitality program begins with core subjects that focus on essential skills in food preparation, kitchen management, customer service, and hospitality operations. Students will learn foundational techniques in food preparation, including cooking methods, food safety and sanitation, menu planning, and presentation. Additionally, subjects on nutrition, beverage management, hospitality law, and customer relations help students understand the broader aspects of the industry. Elective subjects allow specialization in areas such as pastry arts, international cuisines, event planning, restaurant management, and hotel operations. The variety of electives helps students tailor their education to specific areas within the dynamic catering and hospitality industry, preparing them for diverse roles across multiple sectors.

In-house training

Hands-on, in-house training is an essential component of the program, where students gain practical experience by working in fully equipped kitchens, dining areas, and hospitality facilities. They will develop proficiency in culinary techniques, from preparing basic dishes to creating gourmet meals. Students will also learn about kitchen operations, food preparation workflows, and how to manage equipment and tools safely. In addition to food preparation, students practice customer service skills, such as table setting, serving, and managing guest experiences. This training ensures that students not only master the technical aspects of cooking but also understand how to provide excellent service and maintain smooth operations in a hospitality setting.

Work Place Experience learning

Workplace experience is offered through partnerships with local hotels, restaurants, catering companies, and event planners, providing students with real-world exposure. During their placements, students will work in professional kitchens, restaurants, or hospitality venues where they apply their classroom knowledge to actual work environments. This on-the-job learning includes preparing meals for guests, managing service teams, and interacting with customers in a variety of settings. Students will gain valuable insights into the operational demands of the industry, such as time management, teamwork, and maintaining high standards of hygiene and service. These practical experiences also allow students to build industry connections and gain confidence in their ability to manage both food and guest service effectively.

Certification

Upon successful completion of the program, students earn qualifications recognized by Ghana’s Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) framework. The National Proficiency I and II certifications validate skills in basic food preparation, kitchen operations, and customer service. National Certificates I and II further develop these skills, focusing on more advanced culinary techniques, menu planning, and restaurant or hotel management. The Higher National Diploma (HND) prepares graduates for supervisory or managerial roles, such as Head Chef, Restaurant Manager, or Catering Coordinator. These qualifications are recognized both within Ghana and internationally, allowing graduates to pursue employment opportunities both locally and abroad in a range of hospitality settings.

Job Opportunities

Graduates from the Catering and Hospitality program have numerous employment opportunities across various sectors within the hospitality industry. Entry-level positions include roles such as Chef’s Assistant, Waitstaff, Catering Assistant, or Front Desk Receptionist. As students gain experience and additional certifications, they can move into higher roles such as Sous Chef, Catering Manager, Restaurant Manager, or Hotel Operations Supervisor. The hospitality industry offers a broad range of career paths, with opportunities in restaurants, hotels, resorts, event planning companies, and catering services. Graduates can also explore opportunities in hospitality consulting, food journalism, or even start their own catering businesses. With the growing demand for high-quality service and culinary expertise, the sector offers ample room for career progression and personal development.

Things to Know Before Choosing the Course

This course requires a combination of creativity, stamina, and attention to detail. Students should be passionate about food, hospitality, and working in a fast-paced environment where service quality and timing are key. The program involves physical work, long hours, and the ability to handle multiple tasks simultaneously, especially during busy service times. Students should also have strong interpersonal skills, as customer interaction is a significant part of the job. Safety and hygiene are paramount in the culinary and hospitality industries, so students must be committed to maintaining high standards of cleanliness and food safety. Furthermore, students should be prepared to keep up with industry trends, such as sustainable dining practices and new cooking techniques, as the industry continually evolves. This course is ideal for those who are dedicated to developing both their culinary and service skills and aspire to work in a dynamic, rewarding industry.
