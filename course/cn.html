Couser Overview

Core and Elective Subjuect

The Computer Networking and Hardware program starts with core subjects that build fundamental skills, including computer hardware assembly, network configuration, and operating system management. Students gain proficiency in key technologies such as IP addressing, network protocols (TCP/IP), routing and switching, and network security. Alongside these core subjects, electives allow students to specialize in areas such as cloud computing, cybersecurity, wireless networking, and advanced troubleshooting. This array of electives ensures that students can align their studies with specific career goals, enhancing their expertise in high-demand sectors such as IT support, network administration, and cybersecurity.

In-house training

Hands-on, in-house training is an essential component of the program, where students use industry-standard equipment and software tools to simulate real-world networking scenarios. In the school’s IT labs, students gain practical experience with hardware installations, configuring routers and switches, building and troubleshooting networks, and performing system upgrades. They are also trained in network monitoring and security protocols. This environment enables students to develop essential technical skills, build confidence in problem-solving, and practice critical safety measures for working with electronic devices and network infrastructure. This practical training ensures that students are well-prepared for the demands of the industry.

Work Place Experience learning

Workplace experience is facilitated through partnerships with local businesses, where students apply their classroom learning in professional settings. During these placements, students work alongside experienced technicians and network administrators, engaging in tasks like setting up and maintaining networks, troubleshooting hardware, and resolving connectivity issues. This exposure to real-world environments helps students understand operational challenges, work in team settings, and refine their customer service skills. By gaining practical experience and networking with industry professionals, students enhance both their technical competencies and professional confidence, making them well-prepared for the workforce.

Certification

Upon completing the program, students earn certifications that are recognized within Ghana’s Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) framework. These certifications, such as the National Proficiency I and II, offer foundational recognition for skills in hardware assembly and basic networking. Advanced certifications like the National Certificate I and II further develop students' abilities in more complex network systems and maintenance tasks. Additionally, the Higher National Diploma (HND) prepares graduates for more senior roles, such as network administrators or IT support specialists. The certification pathway is internationally recognized, offering students opportunities to pursue careers both locally and globally with well-respected credentials

Job Opportunities

Graduates of the Computer Networking and Hardware program have diverse career opportunities across many industries. Entry-level positions such as IT Support Technician, Network Technician, and Help Desk Support allow new graduates to gain valuable experience. With further qualifications and experience, opportunities expand into roles like Network Administrator, Systems Engineer, and IT Security Specialist, where expertise in network management, troubleshooting, and cybersecurity are crucial. Graduates can also pursue roles in growing sectors such as cloud computing, telecommunications, and network design, with potential for high-paying positions and career advancement in both local and international markets.

Things to Know Before Choosing the Course

This course requires strong problem-solving abilities, attention to detail, and an interest in working with technology and networks. Students should be prepared to work in both hardware and software domains, requiring logical thinking, patience, and an ability to troubleshoot complex issues. Safety awareness is essential, especially when working with electrical components and networking hardware. Continuous learning is crucial as the IT field evolves rapidly, with new technologies and trends emerging regularly. The program is ideal for those who enjoy working with technology, solving technical problems, and want to pursue a career that offers varied, dynamic, and evolving opportunities in the tech industry. Additionally, students should consider long-term career goals, as IT roles often require ongoing learning and skill development to stay competitive
