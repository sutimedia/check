Couser Overview

Core and Elective Subjuect

: The Mechanical Engineering Technology (M.E.T.) program begins with core subjects that cover fundamental principles in mechanics, thermodynamics, fluid dynamics, materials science, and mechanical design. Students will develop skills in areas such as machinery maintenance, heat transfer, mechanical drawing, and the use of Computer-Aided Design (CAD) software. Elective subjects allow students to specialize in areas such as robotics, automation, advanced materials, or renewable energy systems. These electives cater to a wide range of industries, including manufacturing, automotive, aerospace, and energy, empowering students to gain both depth and breadth in their knowledge, positioning them for high-demand careers in engineering and technology fields.

In-house training

Hands-on, in-house training is a crucial component of the program, where students work with industry-standard tools, machinery, and equipment in fully equipped workshops and labs. Students practice designing, constructing, and testing mechanical systems, learning how to troubleshoot mechanical problems, and maintaining machinery and equipment. They gain practical experience in areas such as CNC machining, hydraulic systems, welding, and system integration. These in-house projects allow students to simulate real-world engineering challenges, building technical expertise while reinforcing safety procedures. By working with advanced tools and systems under supervised conditions, students gain the confidence and skillset required to succeed in the engineering industry.

Work Place Experience learning

Workplace experience learning is offered through partnerships with local industries, where students gain exposure to real-world engineering environments. During these placements, students apply theoretical knowledge in practical settings, working on-site with professional engineers and technicians. Tasks can include designing mechanical systems, conducting material testing, maintaining production equipment, and participating in system optimization projects. Students have the opportunity to observe industry practices, communicate within teams, and take on responsibilities related to project management, quality control, and process improvement. These experiences provide students with invaluable industry insights, allowing them to network with potential employers and enhance their technical abilities

Certification

Upon successful completion of the program, students earn qualifications recognized by Ghanaâ€™s Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) framework. National Proficiency I and II certifications validate essential skills in mechanical assembly, machinery maintenance, and system design. National Certificates I and II recognize advanced knowledge in mechanical engineering principles, including CAD modeling, advanced materials, and project management. The Higher National Diploma (HND) prepares students for senior roles within the mechanical engineering sector, including positions in design engineering, production management, and process optimization. The certifications are internationally recognized, allowing graduates to pursue career opportunities both locally and abroad in a wide range of industries.

Job Opportunities

Graduates of the M.E.T. program have diverse employment opportunities in various industries, including manufacturing, construction, aerospace, and energy. Entry-level positions such as Mechanical Technician, Machine Operator, and Maintenance Assistant provide graduates with the opportunity to apply their technical skills while gaining valuable work experience. As they accumulate more experience and certifications, career paths expand to roles such as Mechanical Design Engineer, Process Engineer, and Production Manager, where responsibilities in system optimization, quality control, and project management are essential. Additionally, opportunities exist in cutting-edge fields such as robotics, renewable energy systems, and automation, offering the potential for high-paying, innovative career paths.

Things to Know Before Choosing the Course

The M.E.T. program requires strong analytical skills, problem-solving abilities, and a keen interest in mechanical systems. Students should have an aptitude for working with their hands and be comfortable with complex machinery and tools. Safety knowledge is essential, as students will be working with potentially hazardous equipment such as power tools, welding machines, and high-pressure systems. A strong foundation in mathematics and physics is important for understanding the technical concepts covered in the program. Additionally, students should be prepared for continuous learning, as the field of mechanical engineering is constantly evolving with technological advancements. This course is ideal for individuals who are interested in designing, optimizing, and maintaining mechanical systems and machinery, and it offers a rewarding career path with many opportunities for growth and specialization.
