Couser Overview

Core and Elective Subjuect

The Motor Vehicle Engineering program begins with core subjects that build foundational skills, covering topics such as engine mechanics, electrical systems, fuel and exhaust systems, and vehicle safety protocols. Students learn essential diagnostic and repair techniques, blueprint reading, and mechanical design interpretation, enabling them to troubleshoot and maintain a wide variety of vehicles. Elective subjects allow specialization in areas like advanced diagnostics, automotive electronics, and vehicle modifications, tailoring the training to diverse sectors, including commercial vehicles, high-performance automobiles, and off-road vehicles. The variety of electives supports career goals by offering depth in specialized skills while broadening overall automotive knowledge.

In-house training

Hands-on, in-house training is a critical part of the program, where students work with industry-standard tools and diagnostic equipment, such as engine analyzers, oscilloscopes, hydraulic lifts, and computerized diagnostic systems. Within the school’s fully equipped workshops, students undertake projects that simulate industry tasks, such as performing engine overhauls, conducting brake system inspections, and calibrating fuel injection systems. This controlled environment allows students to gain confidence in working with complex vehicle systems under supervised conditions. The practical projects help students master precision, efficiency, and safety practices, preparing them for the demands of the automotive industry.

Work Place Experience learning

Real-world experience is provided through partnerships with automotive companies, repair shops, and dealerships. During these placements, students apply their classroom-learned skills in genuine work settings, engaging in tasks like vehicle maintenance, diagnostics, and repair under the guidance of industry professionals. This on-the-job training exposes students to the everyday challenges of motor vehicle engineering, such as meeting client expectations, coordinating with colleagues, and managing repair timelines effectively. By working alongside experienced mechanics and engineers, students gain valuable insights into industry standards, network with potential employers, and enhance both their technical skills and professional adaptability.

Certification

Upon completing the program, students earn a series of certifications recognized within Ghana’s Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) framework. The National Proficiency I and II certifications validate fundamental skills for tasks such as vehicle diagnostics and basic repairs. National Certificates I and II progress to more complex technical competencies, while the Higher National Diploma (HND) prepares students for roles that may involve supervisory responsibilities or specialized expertise in motor vehicle systems. These certifications are respected within both Ghana and international automotive sectors, signaling a level of competence that enables graduates to pursue opportunities in local workshops, corporate fleets, or even international repair facilities.

Job Opportunities

Graduates of the Motor Vehicle Engineering program have a wide range of employment options across the automotive sector. Entry-level roles such as Automotive Technician, Diagnostic Assistant, and Service Mechanic provide hands-on experience. With additional experience and certifications, graduates can advance to roles like Vehicle Inspector, Systems Specialist, and Senior Mechanic, where responsibilities may include quality assurance, system troubleshooting, and team coordination. The demand for automotive expertise also opens opportunities in various industries, from automotive manufacturing and racing teams to fleet management and heavy-duty vehicle maintenance, where skills in precision diagnostics and repair can lead to high-paying and specialized positions.

Things to Know Before Choosing the Course

Motor Vehicle Engineering demands physical endurance, hand dexterity, and an analytical mindset for diagnosing complex systems. Safety is paramount, as working with machinery, tools, and automotive chemicals can involve risks. Students should be prepared for continuous learning to stay updated with advancements in vehicle technology, such as electric and hybrid systems. This course is ideal for those with a keen interest in mechanics and problem-solving and a readiness to commit to a trade that offers a dynamic and evolving career path. Additionally, long-term commitment to skill development is essential, as mastery in the field often requires years of hands-on experience and ongoing education.
