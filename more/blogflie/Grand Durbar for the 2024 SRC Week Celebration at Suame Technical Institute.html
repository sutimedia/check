Grand Durbar for the 2024 SRC Week Celebration at Suame Technical Institute: “Career Awareness in the TVET Space, A Tool for National Development”

May, 2024

The Suame Technical Institute hosted a significant event as part of its 2024 SRC Week Celebration—a grand durbar under the theme “Career Awareness in the TVET Space, A Tool for National Development.” This theme underscores the critical role of Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) in equipping students with practical skills and creating a strong workforce that can drive Ghana’s industrial and economic progress. The event brought together students, educators, industry leaders, and government representatives to explore how TVET education can serve as a pillar of national development.
Purpose of the Grand Durbar The grand durbar aimed to highlight the importance of career awareness in the technical and vocational sector, especially as the demand for skilled workers continues to grow. In Ghana, TVET is increasingly recognized as an essential pathway to reduce unemployment, promote entrepreneurship, and advance industrialization. By aligning education with market needs, the durbar aimed to inspire students at Suame Technical Institute to consider various career paths within the TVET sector and understand how their skills can directly contribute to the nation’s development goals.
Keynote Addresses and Panel Discussions The event featured keynote addresses from distinguished guests, including TVET professionals, government officials, and successful entrepreneurs within the technical fields. Speakers emphasized the value of practical skills in driving innovation and meeting the demands of a competitive global economy. Discussions highlighted Ghana’s vision to leverage TVET for sustainable development and job creation, showing students the real-world impact of their studies and future career paths.
One of the main points of discussion was how TVET could solve some of the country’s pressing challenges by promoting self-employment and creating job opportunities within the construction, manufacturing, and services sectors. Experts stressed that a nation’s development depends not only on academic knowledge but on the skills and creativity of its people. By choosing a TVET path, students can play an instrumental role in Ghana’s economic transformation.
Career Guidance and Mentorship Opportunities An essential part of the grand durbar was the career guidance and mentorship session. Representatives from various industries set up booths to offer students insights into different career paths in fields such as automotive technology, electrical engineering, construction, catering, fashion design, and more. This hands-on guidance allowed students to explore various career options and interact with professionals in their areas of interest, providing them with a realistic view of what to expect in their future careers.
Industry mentors shared valuable advice on career progression, skill requirements, and the importance of adaptability in today’s job market. Students were encouraged to embrace lifelong learning, stay updated with technological advancements, and strive for excellence in their chosen fields. The mentorship opportunities offered during the durbar reinforced the importance of TVET education in empowering students to succeed both locally and internationally.
Student and Staff Engagement The grand durbar was also an opportunity for students and staff to actively engage in discussions and share their aspirations and challenges. A lively question-and-answer session allowed students to voice their concerns about their career options, job market uncertainties, and the relevance of TVET in today’s world. Educators were able to address these concerns, highlighting the importance of the skills students are learning and encouraging them to remain focused on their studies.
This interactive segment was particularly empowering for students, as it reassured them that they have a critical role in the nation’s development. Teachers and mentors motivated the students to be innovative, highlighting that TVET-trained professionals are often the driving force behind technological advancements and local industry growth. The engagement fostered a renewed sense of pride among students, reminding them that their efforts in technical education are valuable and appreciated.
Exhibitions and Practical Demonstrations To showcase the skills being developed at Suame Technical Institute, the event included exhibitions and practical demonstrations by students. These displays featured projects across various departments, including electrical engineering, welding and fabrication, fashion design, and hospitality. Students presented their work with enthusiasm, demonstrating the depth of technical knowledge and creativity they have acquired in their studies.
The exhibitions also served as a platform for students to receive constructive feedback from industry professionals, further enhancing their understanding of industry standards. For the visitors and invited guests, the demonstrations were a testament to the high-quality technical education provided at Suame Technical Institute and the potential impact of these skilled individuals on Ghana’s economic growth.
Inspiring the Next Generation of TVET Leaders The grand durbar served not only as an educational event but also as a celebration of TVET and its significance in Ghana’s development journey. By raising awareness about career opportunities in the technical and vocational sectors, the event inspired students to pursue their passions confidently. The speakers and mentors encouraged students to become advocates for TVET and to see their roles in the industry as vital to building a stronger and more resilient nation.
This event also aimed to change the perception of TVET, emphasizing that technical education is not a fallback option but a pathway to rewarding and impactful careers. By addressing these misconceptions, Suame Technical Institute hopes to foster a generation of skilled professionals who are proud of their technical backgrounds and eager to contribute to national progress.
Conclusion The 2024 SRC Week grand durbar at Suame Technical Institute was a resounding success, leaving students, staff, and guests with a renewed appreciation for the importance of TVET education. By focusing on “Career Awareness in the TVET Space, A Tool for National Development,” the event underscored how skilled labor is essential for Ghana’s sustainable growth and industrialization goals. Students left the durbar with a clearer vision of their future and an understanding of how their technical skills can drive positive change in society.
The event reaffirmed Suame Technical Institute’s commitment to producing skilled and knowledgeable graduates prepared to contribute meaningfully to Ghana’s workforce. With TVET as a tool for nation-building, Suame Technical Institute continues to play a pivotal role in shaping the next generation of professionals who will lead Ghana’s journey towards development and prosperity.
